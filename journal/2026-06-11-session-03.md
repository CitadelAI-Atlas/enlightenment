## The headline results

- **The Grof protocol was found in primary form** and our five movements map
  one-to-one onto its five phases, including a "heart music" phase by that
  name. The primary text gives no numeric timings (a "statistical average,"
  adjusted to the room), which validates treating the arc as a normalized
  shape rather than a schedule.
- **H1's premise is now evidence-backed at the group level.** Breathing
  follows musical pacing (Bernardi 2006; Haas 1986), and respiration gates
  tempo's autonomic effects (Watanabe 2015). Our specific slow breath-paced
  envelope during passive listening remains untested: H1 keeps its novelty
  and gains a mechanism.
- **Drumming-theta is honest now**: weak as spontaneous theta-power induction
  (one unpublished n=12 dissertation, one failed replication), emerging as
  frequency-specific neural tracking (2026, n=39, rho=0.39 with altered
  experience). Our rate and our sustained Ascent align with the
  better-supported version.
- **The quiet interval is confirmed novel**: no study anywhere tests silence
  at peak intensity within a session. Bernardi's between-track pause produced
  the lowest arousal of its whole protocol, below baseline. Our trial will be
  designed as a deliberate first test (arousal measures plus absorption,
  present versus absent).
- **Long-form engagement remains genuinely unanswered** even after a
  dedicated search. The player's public session reports may be among the
  first systematic data on it.
