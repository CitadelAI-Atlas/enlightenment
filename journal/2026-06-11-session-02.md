## What the evidence changed

1. **The Peak was redesigned.** Grocke's phenomenological work found pivotal
   moments in receptive music therapy coincide with slow, stable, predictable,
   consonant music, not climactic novelty. v1 moves the harmonic tension crest
   into the late Ascent (42 percent) and holds the Peak stable. This is the
   single largest design change to come out of the research.
2. **A second contour shipped.** The Copenhagen Music Program (Messell et al.
   2022) is descent-heavy: roughly 15 percent ascent, 24 percent peak, 61
   percent descent. Contour shape (standard versus descent-weighted) is now an
   experimental variable, generated by remapping so the two differ in exactly
   one way.
3. **Mechanism language corrected.** Pooled behavioral effects of auditory
   beat stimulation are real (two meta-analyses, g=0.40 and 0.45, longer
   exposure a significant moderator) but EEG entrainment is unsettled (5
   studies for, 8 against, 1 mixed). We no longer say "entrainment" as a
   mechanism claim.
4. **Two of our founding assumptions turned out to be unverified.** The
   ceremonial-drumming theta overlap and the music-paces-breath premise both
   came back "evidence not located." The drumming rate is relabeled as
   traditional precedent. H1 is now flagged as genuinely novel: we may be
   among the first to test its premise at all.
5. **The quiet interval would be a first.** A 2025 systematic review found no
   controlled study of silence at peak intensity within a program; the
   evidence supports silence after music instead. The interval stays, labeled
   exploratory, and the listening protocol now adds post-program silence.

## Honest accounting

Nothing we believed was refuted outright, but the evidence base shifted under
three claims we had graded too generously, and the strongest clinical evidence
transfers to our setting by analogy, not directly. The report says so plainly.
