## The three traditions we synthesize

### 1. Bonny: Guided Imagery and Music (GIM)

Helen Bonny's receptive music-therapy programs are built on **affect-contour
theory**: a session is an intensity profile, with local tension and release cycles
nested inside one master arc. Key import for us: intensity is multidimensional
(harmonic tension, register, density, dynamics) and the master arc peaks past the
midpoint, not at it.

### 2. Grof: holotropic breathwork music protocol

The closest precedent for extended, arc-structured listening. Canonical phase
structure:

1. Opening rhythmic, driving music: builds activation through the early session
2. Breakthrough music: peak intensity
3. "Heart music": warm, emotional, melodic
4. Meditative, integrative music: grounding, spacious, quiet

Critical insight we adopt: **rhythm drives the ascent but interferes with surrender
at the peak**. Percussion is front-loaded, melody back-loaded.

### 3. Receptive music in clinical research

- **The Johns Hopkins session program** (assembled by W. Richards for clinical
  research sessions): a supportive Western classical spine; a deliberate
  near-silence at the most intense point; a sense of vastness created with
  harmonically open, low-rhythm material.
- **Kaelen et al. 2018, "The hidden therapist"** (Imperial College London): in
  supervised clinical sessions, music's perceived resonance and evocativeness
  predicted outcomes better than genre, and poorly fitting music actively harmed the
  experience. Design implication: avoid anything jarring, culturally narrow, or
  demanding.
- **Wavepaths** (Kaelen): argues continuous adaptive music outperforms discrete
  tracks in care settings. Supports our gapless single-program format.

## Entrainment evidence: an honest summary

- **Binaural beats**: real but modest evidence (Garcia-Argibay et al. 2019
  meta-analysis: small-to-medium effects, stronger with longer exposure, which
  favors extended programs). Mechanism: frequency-following response to the
  interaural difference tone.
- **Isochronic tones**: stronger cortical entrainment in EEG studies (the on-off
  amplitude modulation is a more salient stimulus), less studied for subjective
  effects.
- **Ceremonial drumming rate**: the traditional 3-4.5 beats-per-second range
  overlaps the theta band. This is among the oldest entrainment practices and is
  our Movement II backbone.
- **Slow-paced breathing** (about 6 breaths per minute): the best-evidenced
  intervention in this entire document. It reliably shifts autonomic balance
  (heart-rate variability), and listeners entrain breath to musical phrasing
  without instruction. This is our highest-confidence active ingredient.
- **432 Hz tuning and Solfeggio frequencies**: no controlled evidence of specific
  effects. Included only as blindable experimental variables. We make no claims.

## Target brainwave bands

| Band | Range | Associated state | Where we use it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alpha | 8-13 Hz | relaxed awareness | Movements I and V (entry and return) |
| Theta | 4-8 Hz | deep meditation, hypnagogia | Movements II-IV (the session core) |
| Gamma | 30-50 Hz | reported peak and insight states | brief shimmer overlays at the peak only |
