# Arc Specification v1 (FROZEN)

*Frozen 2026-06-11 after verification pass one (docs/02-research-report.md).
Machine-readable form: `arc-v1.json` (standard) and `arc-v1-descent.json`
(descent-weighted variant). Changes to this spec now require a new version
number and a journal entry stating why.*

The arc is a normalized contour: all positions are percentages of total
session duration. The program is rendered at multiple candidate medium-length
durations; duration is an experimental variable.

## Changes from v0, with evidence basis

1. **Peak stability principle** (Grocke, via docs/02): harmonic tension now
   peaks at the 42 percent mark, in the late Ascent, and declines through the
   Peak. The Peak holds stable, consonant, predictable material; no new
   musical elements are introduced there. v0 had tension peaking at the
   session midpoint.
2. **Contour shape is now an experimental variable.** Two frozen contours
   ship: the standard arc below, and a descent-weighted remap (shorter
   ascent, earlier peak, long descent) informed by the Copenhagen Music
   Program proportions (Messell et al. 2022). Generated by
   `scripts/make_descent_spec.py` so the two differ in exactly one way.
3. **Evidence labels corrected** (docs/02): the beat-stimulation layer is no
   longer described as producing entrainment (mechanism unsettled); the
   drumming rate is traditional precedent, not a verified band overlap; the
   quiet interval is explicitly exploratory and would be among the first
   controlled tests of silence at peak intensity.

## Standard contour: five movements, gapless

| # | Movement | Span | Function | Beat stimulation | Character |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Threshold | 0-13% | settle, safety, body | alpha 10 to 8 Hz | warm drone, sparse; breath layer pacing from resting rate toward slow |
| II | Ascent | 13-37% | build, dissolve ordinary attention | theta 8 to 5.5 Hz | percussion enters at 3-4.5 beats per second; density (not tempo) accelerates; harmonic tension crests at 42%; 3:4:5 polyrhythm blur in the final third |
| III | Peak | 37-60% | openness, vastness, surrender | theta 5 Hz | rhythm falls away; stable, consonant, predictable; maximum spatial width; brief near-silence window near the midpoint (exploratory) |
| IV | Heart | 60-80% | warmth, meaning | theta to alpha, 5 to 8 Hz | first true melody: the leitmotif blooms; voices re-converge to unison |
| V | Return | 80-100% | grounding, integration | alpha 8 to 10 Hz | theme returns simplified; field collapses to warm center; taper to silence at the close |

Descent-weighted variant boundaries: 0-9%, 9-27%, 27-45%, 45-70%, 70-100%.

## Structural rules (unchanged from v0)

1. No event is abrupt; transitions span roughly 1.5 to 3 percent of duration.
2. Intensity is density plus harmonic tension plus register, never loudness
   spikes.
3. Rhythm front-loaded, melody back-loaded (traditional practice; primary
   source still unverified, see docs/02 Q1).
4. Nested tension and release cycles inside the master contour (Bonny).
5. Beat-frequency ramps are continuous, never stepped.

## Singular events (unchanged from v0)

Heartbeat opens and closes the program; one rain swell late in the Ascent;
one bell in the Peak; the exploratory quiet interval near the midpoint; the
unison re-convergence in the Heart.

## Listening protocol additions (from docs/02)

- Several minutes of silence after the program ends, which is where the
  controlled evidence for silence actually sits (Bernardi et al. 2006;
  Stolterfoth and Pfeifer 2025).
- Headphones required for the binaural variant; on Apple headphones, disable
  Spatialize Audio and head tracking (see docs/05).
